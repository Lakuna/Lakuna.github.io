Dungeons of the Necromancer's Domain

Port of DND to DOS by Bill Knight in 1984. DND, in turn, was a port of dnd to multiple systems by Daniel Lawrence in 1976. The original dnd was developed by Gary Whisenhunt and Ray Wood in 1974.

dnd is the first interactive game to include bosses.

dnd is the second-earliest surviving role-playing video game, behind only pedit5 (which was never ported to DOS). pedit5 is believed to have been preceded as a role-playing game only by a game called m199h, but no copies of that game survive.

dnd drew inspiration directly from Dungeons & Dragons, which was released earlier in the year it began development.

Oubliette

This version is a port of the original Oubliette to DOS by Bear Systems in 1983. The original Oubliette was inspired directly by Moria, and was developed in 1977 by Jim Schwaiger, John Gaby, Bencherd DeLong, and Jerry Bucksath. Moria was developed in 1975 by Kevet Duncombe, and was influenced by dnd, which was developed in 1974 by Gary Whisenhunt and Ray Wood.

QuickBasic 4.5 EN

Microsoft's version of the original BASIC, developed in 1964 by John Kemeny and Thomas Kurtz.

Rogue: Exploring the Dungeons of Doom

This version is a port of the original Rogue to DOS by Epyx in 1984. The original game was written for Unix-based mainframe computers in 1980.

Rogue was the defining game for the Roguelike genre.
